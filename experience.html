Business Analyst Intern

Capital One FINANCIAL

McLean, Virginia

Summer 2023





What I Did

As credit policy analyst, I was given access to databases that contained information on user attributes and behavior. I was able to utilize SQL to organize millions of entries and to pull insights from the data. By looking at credit policy applications in combination with customer behavior, I was able to identify specific attributes that often pointed towards a customer being more risky (prone to delinquency or charge off). I noticed that specific channels drew in more customers with unfavorable behavior, and if we were to tighten policy in these specific channels, we could reduce risk to the company and increase profitability.



My Advice To Others

Your first internship will challenge you in ways you would never expect. No classroom experience can truly compare to having to practically apply concepts to real life projects and challenges. Prepare to be uncomfortable, prepare to make mistakes, and prepare to grow. There is no “failing” at your first internship, because everything you do (no matter how successful) is a new experience under your belt and a learning opportunity!



What I Learned

This internship was my first experience working in a corporate setting and it was great learning how to function in this environment. During my time in the office I was able to connect with amazing meteors and learn from their professional experiences. My biggest takeaway was that your background does not determine your future. I met so many successful analysts that came from backgrounds such as engineering, economics, and investment banking. Through these conversations, I gained the confidence and perspective to pivot from pursuing careers in analytics to exploring the field of product management.

Teaching Assistant | Intro to Information Systems

University of Maryland

College Park, Maryland

Spring 2022



What I Did

I served as the primary point of contact for a class of 60+ students. As a TA I graded assignments, proctored exams, and held office hours. During my time working with the class I was able to help raise project averages by 20% through my office hour instruction and review sessions.




My Advice To Others

Go to office hours! I know everyone will give this advice to new students, but I say this again in hopes that someone reading this will take my advice. As a TA, I spent most of my time sitting alone in my office because students would not come in until the week a project was released. The teaching staff wants to help you, so do not be afraid to reach out and utilize us as a resource . Your TAs can advise you on things such as internships and class scheduling, conversations don't have to be limited to only homework. I really wish I got the opportunity to connect with more students while working in this position.




What I Learned

One has not truly mastered something until they can teach it. Finishing the class with a near perfect grade, did not prepare me in any way to be a teacher. Through working in this position, I learned that being a teacher is a collaborative experience. Everyone learns in different ways, and there are multiple ways to approach a problem. By working one on one with students you gain insights into creative and innovative ways to solve a problem that you would not have otherwise considered. Through teaching in this way, you also empower students to think outside the box and ensure that everyone feels as though their opinions are valued.

Social Chair

ACES Honor College

College Park, Maryland

Fall 2020 - Spring 2022





What I Did

I served on the student board of my honors college for two years and it was an extremely rewarding experience. As social chair, I worked to plan events (professional, social, and educational) and establish a sense of community within our living learning program. As social chair I conducted corporate outreach to acquire speakers for events, and handled other logistics. My favorite events that I planned were a Women in Cybersecurity Panel and the first annual ACES talent show. I also worked with our student body to advocate for changes in policy and curriculum.


My Advice To Others

We as students have tremendous power. Especially to those working in student organizations, don’t be afraid to go for the impossible. Do not underestimate the power of alumni connections and do not undervalue the resources your school provides you. If you have the opportunity to get involved in your community by holding a leadership position, it is extremely worth it. It feels great to make a direct impact and give back to those who you are closest with.



What I Learned

My greatest takeaway from this experience was learning how to work collaboratively with others to do what is best for the group as a whole. While working on board, there were many times when there was not a clear best way to do something and at times it was tough navigating through many opinions. I have learned that allowing opportunities for ownership and openness to compromise are key to performing best in group settings.

Cheer Instructor

National Cheer Association

2020 - 2023





What I Did

This role was a combination of the two things I love most: cheerleading and travel. As an NCA instructor, I worked with elementary through high school students across the US. I led summer camps and training sessions where I worked to develop athletic, leadership, and communication skills in students. Every camp was an exciting new venture. I collaborated with coaches and students to tailor curriculum to the needs of each specific team, and helped athletes achieve their goals. While working in this position I have met so many amazing people and I am truly grateful for this experience.



My Advice To Others

Always make time for your passions, especially ones that fall outside of your current career path. Cheerleading has always been the thing that makes me happiest and I have been active in the sport since I was 7, but as I have progressed in my career and education it has become increasingly harder to find time for it. It's important not to lose sight of the things that bring you the most joy, no matter how successful you may become. If you have the opportunity to do the things you love, in any capacity, don't take it for granted.
